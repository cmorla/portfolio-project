Ai of Color

We are an independent research initiative with the goal of including and informing populations traditionally oppressed or minimized by algorithmic decision-making. Seeking to diversify the dialogue, foster innovation, and ensure fairness in machine learning.


We analyze, investigate, and raise awareness on the ways algorithmic decision-making and machine learning techniques can perpetuate systemic oppression and exacerbate discriminatory patterns.

DID YOU KNOW?

Biases in artificial intelligence systems tend to come from biases in the data they are trained on. According to one study, a widely used data set is around 75 percent male and more than 80 percent white.

About 80 percent of AI professors are men.

Women make up just 15 percent of AI research staff at Facebook and 10 percent at Google. The percentage is even lower for black employees at major tech firms, with just 2.5 percent at Google and 4 percent at Facebook and Microsoft.
